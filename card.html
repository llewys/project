Raccoon

The raccoon has very nimble fingers that can twist handles and open doors.

  • Scientific Name: Procyon lotor
  • Average Length: 26-43 in. (head to tail)
  • Average Lifespan: 1.8 to 3.1 years (wild).
  • Habitat: Most of North and Central America. Introduced to Germany, Georgia, Azerbaijan, Armenia, northern Iran, and Japan.

The raccoon is curious, clever, and solitary-- and deceptively cuddly-looking. Recognizable by its striped face and ringed tail, the raccoon owes the success of its population to its ability to live in a wide variety of habitats and to survive on an extremely flexible diet. They have been known to live over 20 years in captivity.
